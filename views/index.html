About Surface Disinfection

Surface disinfection is done several times per day in a medical facility, and yet is a difficult and complicated task to achieve. Learn these basic factors in product selection to make the task a little easier and more effective.

Cleaning and Disinfection

Cleaning removes dirt and debris from surfaces, but doesn't kill any germs left behind. Disinfecting kills pathogens with chemicals. Make sure you are using an EPA-registered disinfectant, not just a cleaner.

Factors That Affect Efficacy

Medical-grade disinfectants kill 99.9999% of pathogens on a surface, but only when the surface remains wet. If the label's kill time (the length of time required to kill the pathogens listed on the product label) is longer than the time it takes to dry, full disinfection has not been achieved. Test samples of new products to determine if they will stay wet long enough to meet their kill times. If you are using quat or bleach chemistry liquids with separate wipers, make sure the wiper fabric does not bind to the active ingredient, which diminishes efficacy.

Other Important Factors to Consider

Check the human safety HMIS rating of your product to ensure it's safe for staff and patients. Contact the product manufacturer to make sure the chemistry will not damage your expensive equipment by requesting a material compatibility list. Test products, especially concentrates, for ease of use. Compare the full cost of using the product (not just the product's case price).
